Koncept

This website is an experiment to bring qualitative methods into an otherwise quantative web. Websites today are designed and created for an average user, something we want to change. If you look around you, you can see that everything has different models, sizes and designs in order to appeal to different types of people. Somehow websites are alone in the fact that they use one single model based on an average user for the whole world's population. Imagine if there were only one type of car model in the whole world.

There are different types of people in the world who likes different things. And so we are trying to reach out to those people, not just the average one. By adapting and personalize the web depending on you, the user, we hope to improve the usability. The idea is to make it a smooth experience for the user by allowing for a quick and easy choice to customize their design. For this purpose we have chosen to team up with the validated qualitative method "Pick-a-mood".

For this experiment we are not basing the adaptation and personalization on a personality type, but rather the state of the user's current mood. We are not always feeling the same, we don't always want the same things. By choosing the mood we hope to appeal to how the users are feeling in the moment to always bring an up-to-date experience, that can vary from moment to moment.

About

Who we are

We are two web development students at Blekinge Institute of Technology, currently in our last year of studies. We are doing this project as a part of our course Research Methodology for Media Technology.

What we do

For this project we are using the method diffraction, a term coined by Donna Haraway, to diffract the average user of the web into personalized user profiles in order to improve the usability.

Why we do

We are doing this to spark an interest and to make a change. We hope our visions and ideas may inspire others to continue further research on the subject to change the web for the better.
